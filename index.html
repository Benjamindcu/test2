Introduction

Nike, one of the world's leading athletic apparel and footwear companies, has long recognized the importance of sustainability in today's global market. With increasing environmental challenges and a

growing demand for more eco-conscious products, Nike has committed to integrating sustainability into every facet of its business. From sourcing raw materials to product manufacturing and distribution,

the company has made significant strides in reducing its environmental footprint. Nike's sustainability sector focuses on innovation, responsible supply chain management, and reducing carbon emissions,

all while driving progress toward a more sustainable and equitable future for the planet and its communities.

Move to Zero Initiative

At the core of Nike's sustainability efforts is its "Move to Zero" campaign, which aims to achieve zero carbon emissions and zero waste across the company's

global operations. This ambitious initiative encompasses various aspects of Nike's business, including:

  • Reducing carbon emissions across the supply chain
  • Minimizing waste throughout the product lifecycle
  • Transitioning to renewable energy sources
  • Embracing circular economy principles

Sustainable Materials and Innovation

Nike has recognized that materials account for about 70% of its carbon footprint, making this a crucial focus area. The company has made significant progress in incorporating sustainable materials into its

products:

  • Nike apparel labeled as "sustainable materials" contains at least 50% recycled content
  • The company has repurposed the equivalent of 5 billion plastic bottles into high-performance athletic wear
  • Nike is actively working on developing innovative materials with lower carbon footprints

Supply Chain and Manufacturing

Nike's vast global supply chain presents both challenges and opportunities for sustainability. The company is addressing this through:

  • The Supplier Climate Action Program (SCAP), which supports suppliers in reducing their climate impact
  • Encouraging suppliers to increase renewable energy use and set their own climate targets
  • Implementing waste reduction strategies at all levels of the supply chain

Recycling and Circular Economy

Nike has implemented several programs to promote circularity:

  • The Recycling + Donation program accepts used athletic shoes and clothing for recycling or donation
  • Nike Grind, an initiative started in 1992, repurposes old shoes to create new products and surfaces

Water Conservation and Chemical Management

Nike is also focusing on:

  • Reducing water usage, particularly in cotton cultivation and manufacturing processes
  • Eliminating hazardous chemicals from its production processes

By investing in innovation, collaborating with industry partners, and setting ambitious targets, Nike is demonstrating a strong commitment to sustainability. While challenges remain, the company's efforts

are contributing to significant progress in reducing its environmental impact and promoting more sustainable practices in the athletic apparel industry.

Executive Summary

Nike has set ambitious sustainability goals as part of its "Move to Zero" initiative, aiming to reduce its carbon footprint by 63% by 2030 and reach net zero emissions by 2050. Key focus areas include:

  • Materials: 78% of Nike products contain some recycled material. The company is working to increase the use of sustainable materials like recycled polyester.
  • Manufacturing: Nike is improving energy efficiency and increasing renewable energy use in its supply chain.
  • Innovation: Developing sustainable technologies like Flyknit and Nike Forward that reduce waste and carbon footprint.
  • Waste reduction: Diverting 99% of footwear manufacturing waste from landfills.
  • Product circularity: Programs like Reuse-A-Shoe and Nike Refurbished to extend product lifecycles.

While Nike has made progress, it still faces challenges in areas like chemical use and supply chain transparency. The company is rated as "It's a Start" for sustainability by Good On You, indicating room

for improvement.
